THOMAS J. WATSON SR.

American businessman Thomas John Watson Sr. led IBM as its chairman and CEO. From 1914 through 1956, he managed the company's expansion into a global power. John Henry Patterson's training at NCR influenced Watson in creating IBM's management philosophy and organizational culture. He transformed the business into a highly successful sales organization that was heavily dependent on punched card tabulating equipment. He was a self-made industrialist who, upon his death in 1956, was hailed as the world's greatest salesman and one of the wealthiest individuals of his time.

  • Thomas Watson, Jr. succeeded his father as IBM chairman and later served as ambassador to the Soviet Union under Jimmy Carter
  • Jeanette Watson Irwin married businessman John N. Irwin II, later ambassador to France
  • Helen Watson Buckner became an important philanthropist in New York City
  • Arthur K. Watson served as president of IBM World Trade Corporation and later, as ambassador to France
Birth February 17, 1874
Death June 19, 1956 (aged 82)
